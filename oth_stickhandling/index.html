Program Design

How This Works.

Confidence with the puck is a product of specific repetitions done in the right order. Each level builds the hands the next level demands. Check off each drill when you can do it eyes-up at game speed — that is the only standard that counts.

Top hand first

Your top hand controls 80% of every stickhandling movement. The Top-Hand Only drill builds this faster than anything else. Run it every session, even after you advance.

Slow before fast

Every move has a speed progression. Master the motion slowly and cleanly before adding speed. Fast and sloppy wires the wrong habit — you will have to un-learn it.

Eyes up

Gather information to track defenders and see passing lanes. Depending on the situation you will scan closer or further away. In traffic you look just beyond your stick to see immediate threats. When you have more time you are able to look further ahead. Do not stare at the puck. Slow down before you look down. Over time the speed will come.

Tricks build confidence

The pickups, juggles, and windmill are not decoration. They build spatial awareness, soft hands, and the blade feel that makes all your other moves sharper and more automatic.

Mastery standard

Complete every drill eyes-up at game speed. Try to be smooth and efficient. This only comes through repetition.

Video on every drill

Each drill card includes a video demonstration. The videos can be watched directly from this site or you can watch them on YouTube. Don't forget to subscribe to One Time Hockey on YouTube.
